How is your tax calculated?

As an individual the amount of tax you pay can be summarised with this equation
Total Tax = Income Tax + Medicare Levy

Medicare Levy is simply 2% of your Taxable Income unless your income is below a certain threshold (for details visit this link) in which case you don't have to pay a Medicare Levy. Income tax on the other hands follows a so called "progressive" system. In a progressive system income tax are calculated based on brackets and income within each bracket is taxed at a different rate. Of course the higher the income the higher the tax rate, hence the name progressive. The rate at which each bracket is charged at is called the marginal rate.
Income Tax = Total Previous Bracket Tax + (Taxable Income - Last Bracket Threshold) * Marginal Rate

The below is the Australian income tax rates for the financial year 2017/2018 (i.e. Jul 2017 to Jun 2018).
Income Tax Bracket Marginal rate Total Previous Bracket Tax
$0 - $18,000 0% NA
$18,001 - $37,000 19% $0
$37,001 - $87,000 32.5% $3,572
$87,001 - $180,000 37% $19,822
$180,001 - Infinity 45% $54,232

Tax & Pay Calculation Explained via an example

Consider someone with a taxable income of $78,000. Her income falls into the third bracket, the last bracket threshold is $37,000, and her marginal rate is 32.5% i.e. every dollar that she earns above $37,000 is taxed at 32.5%. But how much tax does she pay for her earnings below $37,000? That is the "Total Previous Bracket Tax" which is $3,572. Bringing everything togehter and her income tax is calculated as
Income Tax = $16,897 = $3,572 + ($78,000 - $37,000) * 32.5%

To work out her take home pay she would need to
Take Home Pay = Taxable Income - Income Tax - Medicare Levy

therefore
Take Home Pay = $59,543 = $78,000 - $16,897 - $78,000 * 2.5%

To convert her take home pay to monthly, fornight, or weekly amounts, simply divided it by 12, 26, or 52 respecitvely
